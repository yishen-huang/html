Dongpo Pork

History

Dongpo Pork, or Dongpo Rou, is a classic dish from Hangzhou, China. Named after the famous poet and statesman Su Dongpo, the dish features braised pork belly that is tender and flavorful.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork belly
  • 100 ml Shaoxing wine
  • 100 g rock sugar
  • 4-5 slices ginger
  • 2-3 green onions
  • Soy sauce to taste
  • Water

Instructions:

  1. Blanch the pork belly in boiling water for a few minutes, then drain.
  2. In a pot, heat a bit of oil and dissolve the rock sugar to create caramel.
  3. Add the pork belly to the pot, then add the Shaoxing wine, ginger, and green onions.
  4. Pour in enough water to cover the pork, then add soy sauce to taste.
  5. Cover and simmer on low heat for about 1.5 to 2 hours until the pork is tender.
  6. Serve hot, preferably with steamed rice.

Hamburger

Description

The hamburger is a popular American dish consisting of a cooked patty of ground meat, usually beef, placed inside a sliced bread roll or bun.

Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 500 g ground beef
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 egg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Bread crumbs
  • 4 hamburger buns
  • Your choice of toppings (lettuce, tomato, cheese, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. In a bowl, combine ground beef, chopped onion, egg, salt, pepper, and bread crumbs.
  2. Form the mixture into patties.
  3. Preheat a grill or pan to medium-high heat.
  4. Cook patties for about 5-7 minutes on each side, until fully cooked.
  5. Place cooked patties in hamburger buns and add your favorite toppings.
  6. Serve hot.
